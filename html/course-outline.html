Course Outline

Our comprehensive 50-hour training program is divided into 25 Zoom sessions covering theoretical aspects and 25 in-person sessions focusing on practical application. Each session lasts one hour. Here's a summary of the course content:

  1. Introduction
    • Review of student background and setting expectations through a questionnaire.
    • Discussing motivations and goals for pursuing a modeling/acting career.
    • Setting realistic and achievable expectations.
    • Conducting a SWOT analysis to identify strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
    • Learning to navigate opportunities and avoid common industry pitfalls.
    • Understanding the key factors to look for in projects: Project, Pay, and People.
  2. The Beginner Strategy
    • Introduction to fashion modeling: types of modeling, requirements, and key elements of strong photos.
    • Practice sessions on emotions, posing, posture, walking, and commercial and audition techniques.
    • Introduction to acting, including line memorization, monologues, and acting techniques, Scene Study, Character Analysis and much more.
    • Voice acting for commercials, character voices, and service industry announcements.
    • Practical skills such as makeup, hair, styling, grooming, fitness, diet, confidence, dance, sports, social media leverage, content creation, and entrepreneurship basics.
  3. Preparation: Practical Training
  4. Advanced Strategy
    • Building a model and actor tool-kit: portfolios, headshots, resumes, demo reels, and more.
    • Guidance on agency submissions, finding the right agency, and preparing for interviews.
    • Collaborating with photographers and getting work published in magazines.
    • Understanding contracts, negotiating payments, and creating mood boards.
    • Learning about the business side of modeling and acting, various markets, unions, and casting platforms.
    • Strategies for getting credits, choosing strong photos, and organizing packages.
  5. Steps in the Right Direction: The 10X Strategy
    • Revisiting strategies, coping with success and failures, finding inspiration, and maintaining consistency.
    • Recommendations for photographers and agencies. Demo Interviews to prepare you further.
    • Real-life training and assistance with styling and posing on photoshoot days.
    • There a ton of add-ons to support you along the way. See the Add-ons Page.
